Public boundary

What to say, and what to avoid

The public surface should be confident about observable benchmark behavior and conservative about private R012-E claims.

Say

Harness design changes agent behavior

Public pages can explain pressure, clean-room controls, self-monitoring, and benchmark output differences with links to supporting runs.

Avoid

Treating internal R012-E data as public research

The gated vault states that R012-E benchmark data is internal self-evolution reference only. The public site should preserve that line.

Operator notes

Maintenance handoff

Keep the public pages readable first. Readers should not need to parse raw markdown ledgers before they understand the claim and caveat structure.

Content

Keep claims tied to artifacts

When a new benchmark is added, update the dashboard and evidence map together so public claims stay traceable.

Design

Use restrained product UI

Prefer compact sections, quiet cards, real figures, and clear tables that make evidence easy to scan.

Safety

Keep the vault boundary explicit

The vault can be linked as gated provenance, but internal-only benchmark material should not be copied into public pages.
